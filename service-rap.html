Resettlement Action Plans (RAP / ARAP)

Culturally sensitive and legally compliant resettlement planning in accordance with World Bank OP 4.12 and Mozambican land law, ensuring fair treatment and livelihood restoration for affected communities.

Home› Services› Resettlement Plans

What We Do

When projects require land acquisition or displacement, a well-designed Resettlement Action Plan (RAP) is both a legal obligation and an ethical responsibility. We prepare RAPs and Abbreviated RAPs (ARAPs) that protect community rights, satisfy funder requirements, and give projects the social license to proceed.

  • Census and socioeconomic baseline surveys of project-affected households
  • Asset inventory and independent asset valuation
  • Eligibility and entitlement framework development
  • Abbreviated Resettlement Action Plans (ARAP) for minor displacement
  • Full Resettlement Action Plans (RAP) for significant displacement
  • Livelihood restoration programmes and income recovery planning
  • Community consultation and participation throughout the process
  • Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) design and implementation support
  • Gender-Based Violence (GBV) and SEA prevention mechanisms
  • RAP/ARAP implementation monitoring and evaluation

RAP vs ARAP

RAP

Full Resettlement Action Plan

Required when 200 or more persons are displaced, or where physical displacement involves loss of shelter. Includes a comprehensive census, livelihood restoration plan, and community consultation programme.

ARAP

Abbreviated Resettlement Action Plan

Applied when fewer than 200 persons are displaced and there is no physical displacement. Covers asset inventory, compensation entitlements, GRM, and monitoring arrangements.

Our Process

01

Cut-Off Date & Census

Establishment of the cut-off date, household census of all project-affected persons (PAPs), and socioeconomic baseline survey capturing livelihoods, income, and vulnerability indicators.

02

Asset Inventory & Valuation

Systematic inventory of all affected assets — land, structures, crops, trees, and businesses. Independent valuation at replacement cost in accordance with WB OP 4.12 and Mozambican land law.

03

Entitlement Framework

Development of the entitlement matrix defining compensation packages for each category of loss — cash, in-kind, or a combination — together with transition allowances and special provisions for vulnerable groups.

04

Stakeholder Consultation

Structured community meetings, individual household consultations, focus groups with women and vulnerable groups, and formal disclosure in local languages.

05

RAP/ARAP Preparation & Approval

Preparation of the full RAP/ARAP document, submission to the project owner and funder for review, and support through regulatory disclosure and approval.

06

Implementation & M&E

Support to project owner during RAP implementation: payment verification, monitoring of livelihood restoration, GRM case management, and completion reporting.
