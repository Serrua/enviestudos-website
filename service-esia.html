What We Do

Our flagship service delivers comprehensive Environmental and Social Impact Assessments (ESIA) for projects across infrastructure, mining, energy, water, and agriculture sectors. Every study is grounded in Mozambican environmental law (Lei n.º 20/97 and Decreto n.º 54/2015) and aligned with the safeguard requirements of international development banks.

  • Preparation of Instruction Process for environmental categorisation
  • Pre-feasibility studies and Environmental Scoping Reports
  • Terms of Reference (ToR) preparation and regulatory submission
  • Baseline environmental and socioeconomic surveys
  • Community and stakeholder public consultation meetings
  • Impact identification, prediction, and significance evaluation
  • Preparation of the final ESIA Report including ESMP
  • Follow-up support during regulatory review and approval

Our Process

01

Screening & Categorisation

We assess project characteristics against regulatory thresholds to determine the appropriate category and level of environmental study required under Decreto 54/2015.

02

Scoping & Terms of Reference

We identify key environmental and social issues, define the study boundary, and prepare the ToR for submission to ANAMA or the relevant provincial authority.

03

Baseline Data Collection

Multidisciplinary field surveys covering ecology, hydrology, soils, air quality, noise, socioeconomics, and cultural heritage across the project area of influence.

04

Impact Assessment

Systematic identification, prediction, and evaluation of positive and negative impacts using established matrices and significance criteria aligned with funder standards.

05

Public Consultation

Structured community and stakeholder consultation meetings, with multilingual summaries and formal minutes prepared in accordance with Mozambican consultation requirements.

06

Report Preparation & Regulatory Support

Clear, comprehensive ESIA Report with integrated ESMP. We support you throughout regulatory review, respond to DPCA queries, and track the Licence Ambiental to issuance.

Standards & Frameworks

All our ESIAs are prepared to satisfy the applicable international standards alongside Mozambican national law:

World Bank OP 4.01 AfDB ISS JICA Guidelines EU EIA Directive Decreto 54/2015 IFC Performance Standards
